Smart Parking Navigation System

Project Proposal

Introduction

Urban congestion due to limited parking availability is a major challenge in densely populated cities such as Osaka and Tokyo. This project proposes the development of a standalone smart parking guidance application that integrates historical traffic data with real-time parking availability collected through web scraping. By optimizing route recommendations based on both traffic conditions and parking occupancy, the system aims to reduce search time, ease congestion, and support sustainable urban mobility in smart cities.

Background and Problem Statement
Parking shortages remain a persistent urban issue in Japan. In major cities such as Osaka, limited on-street parking forces reliance on off-street facilities, often leading to extended searches for available spaces and increased traffic congestion. According to the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism (MLIT), drivers in major Japanese cities spend an average of over 15 minutes searching for parking during peak hours [1]. Although this number does not sound very long, it can definitely be shortened with an introduction of an effective application. To combat this issue, there are websites which attempt to update parking information in real time. For example, iPosNet is a website that tries to provide parking availability in real-time, however the reality is poor [10]. Websites like these fail to actually update information in real-time, hence misleading users to believe that certain parking lots are actually open. These websites are actually quite hard to navigate as they simply post the parking information with minimal UI design. Furthermore, these websites do not necessarily contain information that is pivotal for drivers such as the actual route to get to the parking lot. When we take a look at the official parking website for Times [4], it becomes clear that parking information is provided vividly by showing the parking lots on the map. However, the actual route is not provided, and when we consider how narrow and confusing many Japanese streets are, this may be problematic. Furthermore, an issue that can not be ignored in a country like Japan is the traffic. According to Nashinari Katsuhiro of Tokyo University, the amount of traffic in Japan is so large that its economic damage equates to 1/7 of the national budget [6]. Additionally, according to the Osaka government, there are 327 major traffic congestion points in normal roads, a number higher than many other prefectures [9]. Although traffic is a prominent issue all around Japan, it becomes clear that it is especially bad in a major prefecture such as Osaka as neighboring prefectures in Hyogo and Kyoto have significantly less major traffic congestion points [9]. It is clear that route recommendation applications should not be ignoring these numbers considering how prominent they are. This inefficient search for parking not only exacerbates traffic congestion but also increases fuel consumption and driver stress. Research by Ponnambalam and Donmez demonstrated that searching for parking significantly elevates drivers' cognitive workload and stress levels, as evidenced by changes in driving behavior, reduced speed, and altered glance patterns during parking searches [2]. Existing navigation systems typically direct users to the nearest parking lot based on distance alone, without factoring in real-time occupancy or traffic conditions. As a result, drivers may arrive at fully occupied facilities, further contributing to unnecessary traffic buildup. Our project aims to address these limitations by offering a smart system that integrates live parking availability with historical traffic data, enabling dynamic route adjustments. Additionally, features such as parking duration tracking will assist users in managing parking time and estimating potential costs, creating a smoother, more efficient parking experience.
Vision

We aim to create a standalone smart parking application leveraging historical and real-time data to guide drivers efficiently to available spaces, reducing cruising time, minimizing congestion, and contributing to smarter, sustainable cities.

Objectives
  • Develop a standalone app recommending parking lots based on destination, real-time availability, and historical traffic patterns.
  • Integrate real-time parking data through web scraping and open traffic datasets.
  • Implement dynamic routing algorithms adjusting paths based on conditions.
  • Provide a clear, user-friendly interface.
  • Reduce environmental impact by lowering congestion and idle time.
Features
  • Real-Time Parking Availability: Updates nearby parking availability using APIs and web scraping.
  • Smart Navigation: Dynamic route adjustment based on traffic and parking occupancy.
  • Parking Duration Tracking: Track parking time and estimate real-time costs for stress-free departures.
Methodology

This application will optimize routes to available parking lots, accessible via smartphones. Following the Apache Cordova deployment model, the app ensures accessibility on Android and iOS platforms. Users share their location and are shown available parking lots nearby, focusing initially on "Times Parking" facilities by Park24 Co., Ltd.

Initially focused on Osaka, the project will gradually expand to all 47 prefectures in Japan, requiring multiple parking data sources to cover nationwide availability.
