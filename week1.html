PBL3 Creative Design: Week 1 Report

Team Overview

In Week 1, our team focused on exploring the core theme of “Smart Cities” by generating individual ideas, evaluating feasibility, and beginning the process of narrowing down to a final group project. Early on, we established a clear communication structure, with the team discussing meeting times, idea evaluation, and group coordination. Regular weekly meetings were decided via vote, with offline meetings scheduled on Thursdays.

Brainstorming and Project Proposals

We began with a brainstorming session where each member proposed a potential smart city-related project based on their interests and real-world problems they observed. These included:

  • A smart parking management system that uses real-time data and historical trends to guide users to available parking spots.
  • A traffic flow optimization app that recommends optimal routes using historical traffic data.
  • A 2D indoor navigation system for complex public spaces such as malls and stations.
  • A multilingual chatbot designed to assist new residents in Ibaraki City and access local services.
  • A smart waste management solution for reducing urban littering through deep learning and positive reinforcement.

Research and Feasibility Evaluation

Following the idea generation phase, members conducted research to support their concepts. We explored technologies such as machine learning for parking prediction, visual and audio markers for the less-abled with navigation, and public data scraping and APIs for chatbot integration. Members also investigated ethical implications, dataset availability, and potential system architectures. Ideas were compared against course requirements, especially data acquisition and visualization feasibility.

Team Infrastructure Development

Parallel to idea development, a group website was created to serve as a hub for sharing documents and project updates. The platform, developed using HTML and CSS, will house our project and development progress. This ensures transparency and smooth internal collaboration.

Narrowing Project Focus

Through collaborative discussions and advice from professors, we began filtering out less feasible projects. By the end of the week, we narrowed our options down to four ideas and planned further reduction to two or fewer by the next meeting. The parking optimization system and traffic routing tools were identified as strong contenders due to clear real-world applicability and access to data.

Conclusion and Next Steps

Week 1 was centered around idea generation, research, role assignment, and infrastructure setup. The team showed strong initiative and collaboration. In Week 2, we plan to finalize our project direction, distribute technical roles based on chosen requirements, and begin the design and data acquisition phase of the project.
