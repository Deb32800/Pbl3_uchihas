Proposal

Vision

Our vision is to create a smart parking guidance system as a standalone application that leverages historical traffic data and real-time parking availability to efficiently guide drivers to available parking spaces. This system aims to reduce urban congestion, decrease time spent searching for parking, and promote sustainable urban mobility within smart cities.

Objectives

   1. To develop a standalone application that recommends optimal parking lots based on current location, destination, and historical traffic trends.
   2. To collect parking availability data by web scraping public parking websites and other open data sources.
   3. To integrate historical traffic data into routing decisions, ensuring realistic travel time estimations.
   4. To minimize cruising-related congestion, emissions, and driver frustration by optimizing both route selection and parking availability.
   5. To create a clear and user-friendly application interface that provides intuitive guidance and relevant information.

Problem Area Overview

Parking availability is a persistent challenge in urban environments, contributing significantly to traffic congestion. In Japan, limited on-street parking forces drivers to rely heavily on parking facilities, often leading to increased congestion and frustration. Current navigation systems generally lack integration of historical traffic data and predictive parking availability, limiting their effectiveness. In order to tackle the mentioned problems, our project focuses on developing a standalone smart parking application that leverages both historical and real-time traffic data to determine optimal routing. By integrating web-scraped parking availability information, the app guides drivers to available parking spaces efficiently, reducing unnecessary driving and contributing to improved urban mobility.​ Key features include routing based on current traffic conditions and parking duration tracking, enabling users to monitor how long their vehicles have been parked. The solution employs proven multi-objective routing algorithms, supported by research in parking allocation and predictive analytics. Our project aligns with the "Mobility and Infrastructure" category of smart city development, aiming to advance smarter and more sustainable transportation systems.​ differences
